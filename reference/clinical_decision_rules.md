# Clinical Decision Rules — Quick Reference

## Emergency / Triage

### Ottawa Ankle Rules
**Use:** Determine need for ankle/foot XR after injury
- XR indicated if:
  - Bone tenderness at posterior edge or tip of lateral malleolus, OR
  - Bone tenderness at posterior edge or tip of medial malleolus, OR
  - Inability to bear weight (4 steps) immediately and in ED
- Sensitivity: ~98% for fracture

### Ottawa Knee Rules
**Use:** Determine need for knee XR after injury
- XR indicated if any:
  - Age ≥55
  - Isolated tenderness of patella
  - Tenderness at head of fibula
  - Inability to flex to 90°
  - Inability to bear weight (4 steps) immediately and in ED

### Canadian C-Spine Rules
**Use:** Determine need for C-spine imaging after trauma
- High-risk factors (any → imaging): age ≥65, dangerous mechanism, paresthesias
- Low-risk factors (any → assess ROM): simple rear-end MVC, sitting in ED, ambulatory, delayed onset neck pain, absent midline tenderness
- If able to rotate neck 45° left and right → no imaging needed

### NEXUS Criteria
**Use:** Alternative to Canadian C-Spine Rules
- No imaging if ALL met: no midline tenderness, no focal deficit, normal alertness, no intoxication, no painful distracting injury

## Cardiovascular

### HEART Score
**Use:** Chest pain risk stratification in ED
- **H**istory (0-2): slightly/moderately/highly suspicious
- **E**CG (0-2): normal/nonspecific/significant
- **A**ge (0-2): <45/45-64/≥65
- **R**isk factors (0-2): 0-1/1-2/≥3 or known CAD
- **T**roponin (0-2): ≤normal/1-3x/≥3x
- Score 0-3: low risk (0.9-1.7% MACE) → consider discharge
- Score 4-6: moderate → observation, serial troponins
- Score 7-10: high risk → admit, early intervention

### CHA₂DS₂-VASc
**Use:** Stroke risk in atrial fibrillation
- C: CHF (1), H: HTN (1), A₂: Age≥75 (2), D: DM (1), S₂: Stroke/TIA (2), V: Vascular disease (1), A: Age 65-74 (1), Sc: Sex female (1)
- Score 0 (male) or 1 (female): no anticoagulation
- Score 1 (male): consider anticoagulation
- Score ≥2: anticoagulation recommended

### Wells Score (PE)
**Use:** Pre-test probability for pulmonary embolism
- Clinical signs of DVT (+3), PE most likely diagnosis (+3), HR>100 (+1.5), Immobilization/surgery (+1.5), Previous DVT/PE (+1.5), Hemoptysis (+1), Malignancy (+1)
- Score ≤4: PE unlikely → D-dimer
- Score >4: PE likely → CTPA

### Wells Score (DVT)
**Use:** Pre-test probability for DVT
- Active cancer (+1), Paralysis/immobilization (+1), Bedridden >3d or surgery <12wk (+1), Localized tenderness (+1), Entire leg swollen (+1), Calf >3cm (+1), Pitting edema (+1), Collateral superficial veins (+1), Previous DVT (+1), Alternative diagnosis as likely (-2)

## Respiratory

### CURB-65
**Use:** Pneumonia severity / disposition
- **C**onfusion, **U**rea>7mmol/L, **R**R≥30, **B**P<90/60, Age≥**65**
- Score 0-1: outpatient
- Score 2: consider short inpatient or supervised outpatient
- Score 3-5: inpatient, consider ICU if 4-5

### PSI / PORT Score
**Use:** Pneumonia severity (more detailed than CURB-65)
- Class I-III: outpatient consideration
- Class IV-V: inpatient

## Hepatology / GI

### MELD Score
**Use:** Liver disease severity, transplant prioritization
- Formula: 3.78 × ln(bilirubin mg/dL) + 11.2 × ln(INR) + 9.57 × ln(creatinine mg/dL) + 6.43
- MELD-Na includes sodium correction

### Child-Pugh Score
**Use:** Hepatic function classification
- Parameters: bilirubin, albumin, INR, ascites, encephalopathy (each 1-3 points)
- A (5-6): well-compensated
- B (7-9): significant functional compromise
- C (10-15): decompensated

## Surgical / Perioperative

### ASA Physical Status Classification
- I: Normal healthy patient
- II: Mild systemic disease
- III: Severe systemic disease
- IV: Severe systemic disease, constant threat to life
- V: Moribund, not expected to survive without operation
- VI: Brain-dead organ donor

### Caprini Score
**Use:** VTE risk assessment for surgical patients
- Score 0-1: low risk → early ambulation
- Score 2: moderate → mechanical prophylaxis
- Score 3-4: high → chemical + mechanical prophylaxis
- Score ≥5: highest → extended prophylaxis
